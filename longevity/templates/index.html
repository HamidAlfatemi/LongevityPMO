LISA (Longevity Intelligence Systems Architecture)

A Dual-Entity Architecture for the Global Longevity Movement

Align academic and industry ecosystems to reduce fragmentation, increase productivity, and accelerate progress in longevity science and interventions.

The global longevity field is entering a new era. Advances in biotechnology, omics sciences, artificial intelligence, and systems biology are rapidly expanding our understanding of aging and age-related diseases. Yet despite this progress, the longevity ecosystem remains highly fragmented, slow to translate discoveries into impact, and inefficient in coordinating efforts across disciplines and stakeholders.
Aging is a multifactorial, system-level biological process. Addressing it effectively requires not only scientific breakthroughs, but also better integration of knowledge, faster translation cycles, coordinated prioritization of efforts, and large-scale productivity gains in research and development. The traditional, linear model of scientific progress is no longer sufficient for the scale and urgency of the longevity challenge.
LISA (Longevity Intelligence Systems Architecture) is proposed as a coordinated response to this reality. LISA is not a single platform or organization, but an ecosystem-level acceleration and coordination architecture. Its purpose is to orchestrate, integrate, and accelerate the global longevity movement by leveraging artificial intelligence, portfolio intelligence, and systems-level thinking.
Rather than attempting to replace existing actors, LISA is designed to empower and connect them — helping researchers, startups, industry, clinics, and investors work more effectively as parts of a coordinated global portfolio against aging.
LISA operates through a dual-entity model that combines a mission-driven scientific pillar with a commercial execution and governance pillar. Together, these pillars aim to increase the productivity, coherence, and strategic direction of the longevity ecosystem.

Pillar A: Longevity Systems (Scientific & Mission Pillar)

The Longevity Systems serves as the scientific integration and legitimacy layer of LISA. Its role is not traditional academic research alone, but high-productivity, AI-leveraged scientific synthesis and framework development.

Its core functions include:

  • Integrating and structuring existing knowledge on aging mechanisms and interventions
  • Developing system-level representations of aging biology (e.g., SBHA and related frameworks)
  • Designing and running high-impact, technology-leveraged research programs
  • Establishing scientific standards, reference models, and shared conceptual frameworks
  • Enabling faster knowledge translation across the ecosystem

Longevity Systems emphasizes scientific acceleration rather than slow theory accumulation, leveraging AI and modern computational tools to multiply research productivity and reduce duplication of effort across the field.

Pillar B: Longevity PMO (Portfolio Governance & Execution Pillar)

The Longevity Portfolio Management Office (Longevity PMO) acts as the orchestration, governance, and execution layer of LISA.

Its primary role is to treat the global longevity effort as an interconnected portfolio of initiatives and to actively support its coordination and acceleration.

Its core functions include:

  • Mapping and monitoring the global longevity portfolio
  • Identifying gaps, bottlenecks, and high-leverage opportunities
  • Initiating enabling and infrastructure projects
  • Coordinating stakeholders and facilitating collaborations
  • Supporting productization and industrialization of key technologies
  • Ensuring financial sustainability through selected monetizable programs

While the PMO can launch and support commercial initiatives, its strategic identity is that of a portfolio orchestrator and ecosystem accelerator, not merely a venture builder.

LISA is designed as a self-reinforcing acceleration system in which value created through ecosystem coordination is reinvested into scientific and knowledge infrastructure. This creates a continuous feedback loop that supports prioritization, integration, and responsible high-impact progress in longevity.
